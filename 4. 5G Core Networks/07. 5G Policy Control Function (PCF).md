# 07. 5G Policy Control Function (PCF)

The **Policy Control Function (PCF)** is the centralized brain of the 5G Core (5GC) responsible for policy decisions and charging control rules. It interfaces with other Network Functions to enforce subscription-based policies dynamically.

![[PCF_Slide_1.png]]

---

## 🗺️ 1. Policy Hierarchy Levels

The PCF operates across a nested hierarchy to dictate rules at different granularities:

![[PCF_Slide_2.png]]

1. **User-level Policies:** Global policies applied to all traffic belonging to a specific subscriber profile.
2. **Service-level Policies:** Specialized policy rules assigned to specific applications used by a subscriber (e.g., free data for voice, or limited speeds for video streaming).
3. **Session-level or Data-flow-specific Policies:** Fine-grained dynamic QoS policies mapped to specific PDU Sessions or individual IP packet streams.

Policies are broadly divided into two major operational branches:
* **Session Management (SM) Related Policies:** Direct QoS and traffic gating controls.
* **Non-Session Management Related Policies:** Roaming, mobility, and routing policies.

---

## ⚡ 2. Non-Session Management Related Policies (Granular Details)

Non-session policies control how the UE accesses the overall network, and are routed via the AMF:

![[PCF_Slide_3.png]]

* **Access and Mobility (AM) Related Policies:**
  * **Service Area Restrictions:** Defines geographical zones (Tracking Areas) where the subscriber is permitted or blocked from registering (e.g. blocking roaming in specific countries).
  * **RFSP (RAT/Frequency Selection Priority) Index:** A priority index (value between 1 and 256) determined by the PCF and sent to the AMF, which forwards it to the gNodeB over the N2 control interface. The gNodeB's scheduler uses the RFSP index to configure specific radio resource priorities, prioritizing cell camping and active handovers on target frequency bands for that specific subscriber class.
* **Policies Delivered Directly to the UE:**
  * **URSP (UE Route Selection Policy):** Crucial 5G rule set sent directly to the UE via NAS N1 signaling. It contains **Route Selection Descriptors** instructing the UE's operating system exactly which PDU Session, network slice (S-NSSAI), and Data Network Name (DNN) to route specific application traffic to (based on App ID, FQDN, or OS ID).
  * **ANDSP (Access Network Discovery and Selection Policy):** Directs the UE on how to discover and select non-3GPP access networks (like Wi-Fi).
  * **Packet Flow Descriptors (PFD):** Exposes application-specific traffic signatures to the SMF/UPF for deep packet inspection.

---

## 🏎️ 3. Session Management Related Policies (Granular Details)

Session policies directly govern packet flow behavior and QoS, and are routed via the SMF:

![[PCF_Slide_4.png]]

* **Gating Control:** Dictates whether to allow or block IP packets belonging to a specific service flow (e.g., blocking unauthorized VoIP traffic).
* **QoS Control:** Authorizes and provisions the specific quality of service parameters for active service data flows.

---

### The PCC (Policy and Charging Control) Rule Structure:
The PCF defines **PCC Rules** and transmits them to the SMF. A single PCC Rule contains:
* **Rule Name:** A unique string identifier.
* **Service Data Flow (SDF) Template:** The packet filters (IP 5-tuples) used to match packets.
* **Precedence:** Determines the evaluation order of the rule.
* **Gate Status:** Open or Closed.
* **QoS Parameters:** Includes the **5QI (5G QoS Identifier)**, **ARP (Allocation and Retention Priority)**, Maximum Bit Rate (MBR), and Guaranteed Bit Rate (GBR).
* **Charging Key:** Directs the billing engine on which tariff to apply.

The SMF splits and translates this PCC Rule into three actionable elements:
1. **SDF Templates:** Installed in the UPF (as PDRs) to identify specific packet streams.
2. **QoS Profiles:** Transmitted to the gNodeB and UPF (via N2 and N4) to reserve bandwidth and assign the QoS Flow Identifier (QFI).
3. **QoS Rules:** Transmitted to the UE (via NAS N1) to instruct the phone's hardware how to map uplink packets to the correct QoS flows.
