# 02. 5G Use Cases and Performance Dimensions

When the ITU mapped out 5G, they realized that a modern network shouldn't just be about making smartphones faster. Different industries need completely different things. To handle this, 5G standardization splits its capabilities into a **three-dimensional triangle of use cases** (defined under the ITU-R M.2083 recommendation).

![[Materials/Slides/UseCases_Slide_1.png]]

---

## 📐 The 5G Use Cases Triangle

A single physical 5G network must accommodate extremely diverse use cases, ranging from ultra-high-speed streaming to delay-intolerant control systems and millions of low-power sensors.

![[Materials/Slides/UseCases_Slide_2.png]]

### The Three Pillars and Intersections:
* **eMBB (Enhanced Mobile Broadband):** Focused on high data rates and wide area capacity.
* **URLLC (Ultra-Reliable and Low-Latency Communications):** Focused on extreme reliability and minimal latency.
* **mMTC (Massive Machine-Type Communications):** Focused on ultra-dense device connectivity and long battery life.

#### Specialized Intersections:
* **eMBB + mMTC:** Smart Home, Smart Building, Smart City.
* **eMBB + URLLC:** 3D Video, Ultra-HD (UHD) Screens, Work and Play in the Cloud, Virtual Reality (VR), and Augmented Reality (AR).
* **mMTC + URLLC:** Industry Automation, Self-Driving Cars, Mission-Critical Applications, and Future IMT Systems.

---

## 📡 1. eMBB (Enhanced Mobile Broadband)

This is the direct evolution of 4G, entirely focused on **high data rates, extreme capacity, spectral efficiency, and user experience**.

![[Materials/Slides/UseCases_Slide_3.png]]

### 📋 Technical Targets:
* **Peak Data Rate:** **20 Gbps downlink (DL)** and **10 Gbps uplink (UL)** per cell.
* **User Experienced Data Rate:** **100 Mbps download (DL)** and **50 Mbps upload (UL)** under normal network loads.
* **Peak Spectral Efficiency:** **30 bps/Hz (DL)** and **15 bps/Hz (UL)** (transmitting 30x more bits per second per Hz of bandwidth).
* **Area Traffic Capacity:** **10 $Mbps/m^2$** in hotspots (allowing dense crowds to stream high-capacity data).
* **Mobility / Speed:** Seamless performance at traveling speeds up to **500 km/h** (e.g., inside high-speed bullet trains).
* **Latency:** User plane latency of **4 ms** (wide-area) to support heavy traffic loads before buffering.
* **Economic Target:** Ultra-low cost per bit and high energy efficiency per bit.

### 📝 Real-World Examples & Environments:
* Next-generation smartphones and connected corporate laptops.
* Dense venue environments (e.g., stadiums, auditoriums, airports) where fans stream live, personalized, multi-angle UHD feeds.
* High-bandwidth applications: 4K/8K UHD streaming, wireless broadcasting, Immersive VR, and AR.

---

## 🔌 2. mMTC (Massive Machine-Type Communications)

Designed to establish communication links between **millions of tiny, low-power devices** that transmit small packets of data very infrequently. This is the foundation of the Internet of Things (IoT).

![[Materials/Slides/UseCases_Slide_4.png]]

### 📋 Technical Targets:
* **Connection Density:** Support for **1,000,000 (1 million) devices** per square kilometer ($km^2$).
* **Battery Life:** Ultra-low power consumption, allowing devices to operate for **10 to 15 years** on just two standard AA batteries.
* **Low Data Rate:** Typically capped between **1 bps to 100 kbps** (only broadcasting small status packets).
* **Latency:** Non-time-critical; latency can range from **seconds to hours** depending on reporting intervals.
* **Enhanced Coverage:** Extra signal boost (e.g., narrow-band extensions) designed to punch through thick concrete and reach deep subterranean spaces.

### 📝 Real-World Examples & Environments:
* **Smart Utility Meters:** Tuck-away gas and water meters in basements.
* **Logistics & Fleet Management:** Low-cost tracking sensors attached to thousands of shipping pallets or cargo container fleets.
* **Smart Agriculture:** Wide-area environmental and soil moisture sensors spread across vast agricultural crop fields.
* **Smart Cities:** Automated trash bins sending 1-bit alerts ("Full" / "Empty") to optimize municipal garbage collection routes.

---

## 🏎️ 3. URLLC (Ultra-Reliable and Low-Latency Communications)

Throws high data rates out the window to focus exclusively on **instantaneous speed, absolute dependability, and zero-lag mobility**. These are for "mission-critical" or "life-critical" applications where a packet drop or network delay could result in system failures or casualties.

![[Materials/Slides/UseCases_Slide_5.png]]

### 📋 Technical Targets:
* **User Plane Latency:** Extremely fast **0.5 ms to 1 ms** end-to-end packet transmission time (point A to B).
* **Control Plane Latency:** Max **10 ms** (time taken to transition a device from idle to active state).
* **Reliability:** **99.999% success rate** (meaning less than 1 out of 100,000 packets fail to deliver).
* **Mobility Interruption Time:** **0.0 milliseconds** (absolute zero handover interruption time when switching between cell towers).
* **Supported Data Rates:** Typically low-to-medium (from **50 kbps up to 10 Mbps**) to ensure packet reliability over raw speed.

### 📝 Real-World Examples & Environments:
* **Autonomous Driving:** V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) safety systems where brakes are triggered instantly.
* **Traffic Control:** Smart traffic control loops coordinating complex intersections.
* **Remote Healthcare:** Haptic remote robotic surgery where a doctor operates on a patient from another continent.
* **Industrial Automation:** High-speed synchronized robots working on factory assembly lines.

---

## 📈 Diverging Requirements Summary (ITU-R M.2083)

Because these three use cases have fundamentally contradicting needs, a single physical 5G network must divide itself. This is achieved via **Network Slicing**—logically partitioning the physical infrastructure into customized virtual networks, each optimized for a specific pillar.

![[Materials/Slides/UseCases_Slide_6.png]]

*Source Reference:* International Telecommunication Union (ITU) Recommendation **ITU-R M.2083** (Vision for IMT-2020 and beyond).
